// Project History

From Two Schools
to the World

What started as a pilot connecting New Zealand and Chile has grown into a global student network uniting young people across the Pacific and beyond — supported by researchers, universities, and government agencies.

OCEAN CONNECT US comenzó como una colaboración entre el Liceo Juan Bautista Contardi en Punta Arenas, Chile y Rosmini College en Auckland, Nueva Zelanda — conectando a estudiantes a través del Océano Pacífico para explorar el cambio climático juntos.

2022

Pilot launched: Rosmini College (Auckland) and Liceo Juan Bautista Contardi (Punta Arenas, Chile). 35 students, inspired by the Winds of Change project.

May 2023

Expanded to include students from South Korea, Marshall Islands, and Hawaii for the first progress-sharing session.

Nov–Dec 2023

First full TOTCUS Symposium. Peru, Washington State, India, Finland, and Singapore joined. All presentations archived in Sharing.

2024

Over 250 students collaborating by August. Second Symposium held with a growing focus on action-oriented projects.

2025

Germany, Vietnam, South Korea, and Philippines joined. Presented at the world Adaptation Futures Conference. East and West Symposiums hosted in November.

2026 →

New partners, maturing projects, focus on Small Island Nations of the Pacific. Two symposiums planned.
